How to analyze Google Ads performance

Google Ads exports are usually cleaner than social exports, but they still need context. Brand and non-brand behave differently, and search often needs to be judged by efficiency and volume together.

Google AdsSearchBrand vs non-brandShopping

Why this matters

  • Brand campaigns can hide weak account-wide efficiency if you look at them in isolation.
  • Non-brand search needs enough conversion volume before you call it a winner.
  • Shopping and performance-focused campaigns can be efficient but still saturated.

Common mistakes

  • Comparing brand and non-brand as if they had the same role.
  • Treating a high-spend campaign as a winner without checking return.
  • Scaling a low-volume campaign before the signal has stabilized.

What strong vs weak signal looks like

  • Strong: efficiency is clearly better than the account baseline and volume is stable.
  • Weak: a campaign looks fine on paper but cannot absorb more budget safely.
  • Thin: the data supports observation, not a big move.

How Gnomeo approaches it

  • Keeps brand and non-brand context separate in the recommendation layer.
  • Prefers controlled increases over aggressive jumps.
  • Uses the audit layer to stop overconfident scaling when the data is narrow.

Why conservative analysis matters

A Google campaign can look “safe” while still being too small, too seasonal, or too dependent on one query cluster. Conservative analysis keeps the recommendation proportional to the signal.
