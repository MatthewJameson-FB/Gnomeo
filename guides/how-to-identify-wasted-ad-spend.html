How to identify wasted ad spend

Wasted spend is usually obvious only after you compare spend against return, consistency, and the rest of the account. Gnomeo keeps that review conservative so you do not overreact to one noisy campaign.

Wasted spendBudget reviewConservativeOperational

Why this matters

  • A campaign can look active and still be quietly wasting budget.
  • High spend with weak return is more important than low spend with weak return.
  • You need context before you call something a cut candidate.

Common mistakes

  • Cutting the first campaign that looks under average.
  • Ignoring the difference between a test and a mature campaign.
  • Using estimated impact as if it were guaranteed.

What strong vs weak signal looks like

  • Strong waste signal: high spend, weak return, and no clear stabilizing trend.
  • Weak waste signal: a campaign is under average, but the volume is too low to act on confidently.
  • Thin signal: the report can point you at risk, but not at an immediate cut.

How Gnomeo approaches it

  • Looks for spend concentration before recommending reductions.
  • Prefers a reallocation when a stronger campaign is already visible.
  • Leaves the audit layer in place so weak evidence stays cautious.

Example recommendation snippet

Sample data only
Reduce £2,400.00 from Meta Prospecting Broad UK → increase Google Brand Search by £2,400.00.

Rationale: the spend is material, but the return is weak enough to justify a controlled reallocation instead of more spend.

Why conservative analysis matters

Wasted spend is useful only if the recommendation is sized correctly. If the data is noisy, it is better to point at the likely waste and keep the move controlled than to overpromise on exact savings.
