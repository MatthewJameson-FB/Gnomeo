Google Ads vs Meta Ads budget allocation

The platforms serve different roles. Search captures intent; Meta often creates and re-captures demand. The budget question is not which platform is “better,” but which signal is stronger for the next pound.

Cross-platformBudget allocationGoogle AdsMeta Ads

Why this matters

  • A good budget move in one platform can be a bad one in the other.
  • Intent, audience stage, and conversion lag matter more than platform loyalty.
  • The account should be reviewed as a system, not as two unrelated dashboards.

Common mistakes

  • Moving budget just because one platform has a higher headline ROAS.
  • Ignoring the role of prospecting versus retargeting.
  • Scaling across platforms before the signal is strong enough.

What strong vs weak signal looks like

  • Strong: one platform has a clearly better return for the same role.
  • Weak: the comparison is distorted by role, audience, or volume differences.
  • Thin: the report can suggest a direction, but not a bold shift.

How Gnomeo approaches it

  • Normalizes Google and Meta exports into one review layer.
  • Looks for the next best budget move, not the biggest possible move.
  • Stays conservative when the platform comparison is not clean.

Example recommendation snippet

Sample data only
Reduce £1,854.00 from Meta Prospecting - Broad → increase Meta Retargeting - Cart/ViewContent by £1,854.00.

Rationale: the signal is better on retargeting, so the move stays controlled instead of broadening spend.

Why conservative analysis matters

Budget allocation works best when it is sized to the signal that is actually present. If the platform comparison is weak, Gnomeo holds back rather than pretending the answer is clearer than it is.
